How to use Posture Pro

Set up once, benefit every day.

Follow these steps to place the Eye-Q Sensor, attach the Mesh-Up, and start receiving gentle, intelligent posture cues while you work.

Setup guide
Detail level
Sensor Mesh-Up Connect Train

Step 1 — Place the Eye-Q Sensor

Position the Eye-Q Sensor on your desk, slightly off-center beneath your monitor, with a clear line of sight to your forehead when you sit normally.

  • Keep it stable and not blocked by objects like notebooks or plants.
  • Angle it gently upward so the sensing window faces your head, not the keyboard.
  • Connect power and confirm the status light indicates it is ready.
Why this matters

Eye-Q measures your head-to-screen distance. A clear view lets it detect when you lean in or drift away, which is the first sign your posture is breaking down.

Step 2 — Attach the Mesh-Up

Wrap Mesh-Up around your office chair backrest, keeping the lower vibration zone aligned with the curve of your lower back when seated.

  • Use the straps to tighten Mesh-Up so it does not slip when you lean back.
  • Check that the haptic modules sit roughly behind your lumbar region.
  • Ensure the cable or power source is routed safely away from your feet and wheels.
Why this matters

The closer Mesh-Up is to your lower back, the clearer each vibration feels as a cue—not a distraction. Proper placement makes the feedback intuitive.

Step 3 — Connect and calibrate

Open the ErgoMatrix app or web dashboard, connect to Eye-Q and Mesh-Up, and run the short calibration flow to match your natural sitting height and screen setup.

  • Follow on-screen prompts to sit upright at your "healthy" distance as a baseline.
  • Choose your preferred sensitivity for posture alerts and vibration intensity.
  • Save your profile so the system recognizes you at this workstation.
Why this matters

Calibration teaches the system what "good" looks like for your body and desk, so alerts trigger only when posture truly deviates from that baseline.

Step 4 — Start your posture training

Work as usual and let Posture Pro run quietly in the background. When Eye-Q detects a slump, Mesh-Up sends a brief vibration to remind you to reset your position.

  • Respond to each cue by gently stacking your head over your shoulders and hips.
  • Check your dashboard occasionally to see your posture streaks and corrections.
  • Pair with short movement breaks every 45–60 minutes for best results.
Why this matters

The small, repeated corrections add up. Over time, your default sitting posture improves, even when the system is not actively reminding you.

Daily posture habits

Desk height
Keep elbows at ~90°

Adjust your chair and desk so your elbows rest near a right angle when typing or using the mouse.

This reduces tension in shoulders and forearms and keeps your wrists from bending sharply, which pairs well with stable sitting posture.

Screen position
Top of screen at eye level

Raise or lower your monitor so the top edge sits near your horizontal line of sight.

Looking slightly downward with your eyes—not your neck—helps keep your head balanced over your spine throughout long sessions.

Movement
Micro-breaks every hour

Stand up, stretch, or walk for 30–60 seconds every 45–60 minutes, even when Posture Pro is active.

Movement lets your discs rehydrate, your muscles reset, and your circulation improve, multiplying the benefits of better sitting form.

Breathing
Use posture to reduce stress

When Mesh-Up nudges you, pair the reset with one slow, deep breath to loosen neck and shoulders.

A calmer breathing pattern plus upright posture can lower perceived stress and help you stay focused longer without feeling drained.
